First, install the app on your phone

1

Download & open

Tap the button above, then open the downloaded .apk file.

2

Allow the install

Android will say it's from an "unknown source" — this is normal for apps outside the Play Store. Tap Settings, turn on Allow from this source, then go back and tap Install.

3

If you see a warning

If Play Protect shows "unsafe app", tap More details → Install anyway. (It flags anything not from the Play Store.)

4

Open & connect

Open PhotoSync, allow photo access, enter your name, and tap Find server while on your home Wi‑Fi. Done — your photos start backing up.

Second, set up the home computer

Your photos go to PhotoSync Server — a small free program on the household's Windows PC that keeps them on your own drive. Set it up once (about a minute) and it then starts on its own every time the computer is turned on.

1

Download & unzip

On the home PC, download PhotoSync Server for Windows below and right‑click the .zip → Extract All. Keep the folder somewhere like Documents.

2

Double‑click PhotoServer

Open PhotoServer inside the folder. If Windows shows "Windows protected your PC", click More info → Run anyway (normal for free apps outside the Store).

3

Choose where photos go

A dashboard opens and a green icon appears by the clock. Click Browse… and pick a folder (an external drive is ideal). That's it — it now runs automatically in the background from every restart.

How it works

Why only on home Wi‑Fi?

Your photos go straight to your family's own computer — not to a company's cloud. Your phone can only reach that computer when both are on the same home Wi‑Fi, so backups pause on mobile data or other networks and resume automatically once you're home. That's what keeps it private and free.

Are my photos on the internet?

No. They stay on your own computer's drive at home. Nothing is sent to the cloud or to us.

Do I have to keep the app open?

No. Once set up, it backs up automatically in the background while you're on home Wi‑Fi. There's also a "How it works" page inside the app if you ever forget the details.
