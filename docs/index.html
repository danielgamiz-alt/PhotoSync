Setup — phone (one time)

1
Tap Download for Android above, then open the .apk file.
2
Android will warn "unknown source" — tap Settings → Allow from this source → Install. If Play Protect asks, tap Install anyway.
3
Open PhotoSync, allow photo access, enter your name, and tap Find server while on home Wi‑Fi.

🛡️ Why you'll see scary warnings — and why it's safe

Those "unknown source" and "protected your PC" warnings appear for every app that isn't sold through Google Play or the Microsoft Store. They're about where the file came from, not whether it's harmful.

PhotoSync is open source: every line is public on GitHub, your photos never leave your home network, and nothing is ever uploaded to us or any cloud. Read the code →

Setup — home PC (one time)

1
Download Server for Windows above and run the installer. If Windows says "protected your PC", click More info → Run anyway.
2
A dashboard opens. Click Browse… and pick a folder for your photos. Done — it starts automatically on every restart.

Your phone backs up while it's on the same Wi‑Fi as the PC and the PC is on.

Common questions

Are my photos on the internet?

No. They go straight to your own PC's hard drive. Nothing is uploaded to any cloud or third party.

Why only on home Wi‑Fi?

Your phone talks directly to your home computer, so both need to be on the same network. Backups pause on mobile data and resume automatically when you get home.

Why doesn't a new photo back up instantly?

Android limits background activity to save battery. Photos trickle in — especially while the phone is charging on Wi‑Fi. To force an immediate backup, just open the app.

Is it really free?

Yes. PhotoSync is free and open source. There's no account, no subscription, and no upsell — your photos live on hardware you already own.
