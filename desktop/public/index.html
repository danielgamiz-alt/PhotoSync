    How PhotoSync works

    PhotoSync backs up your phone's photos and videos straight to your family's own computer at home — not to a company's cloud. That's what keeps it private and free.

    Because the photos go to that computer, your phone can only back them up when both are on the same home Wi‑Fi and the computer is turned on.

    Common questions

    Why does it only work on my home Wi‑Fi?

    Your photos are saved on your own computer at home, not on the internet. For now PhotoSync backs up only while your phone and that computer share the same home Wi‑Fi — it's the simplest and safest way to reach it, and since most people are home every day your photos still get backed up regularly. On mobile data or a different Wi‑Fi — work, a café, a friend's house — backups simply pause and pick up again automatically once you're home.

    Why do I need to add a server?

    Unlike Google Photos, no company is storing your pictures. The "server" is just this free PhotoSync Server app running on your household's computer — that's where your photos are kept. The app on your phone needs its address so it knows where to send them. Tap "Find server" in the phone app while at home and it's usually detected automatically.

    Are my photos uploaded to the internet?

    No. They go only to your own computer on your home network and stay on its drive. Nothing is sent to the cloud or to us.

    Do I need to keep the app open?

    The phone app doesn't need to stay open — once set up, it backs up automatically in the background about every 15 minutes while on home Wi‑Fi.

    PhotoSync Server (this app) does need to be running on the computer, since that's where photos are stored. By default it starts automatically with Windows and runs quietly in the background.

    Why doesn't it upload right away?

    It backs up on its own — but not always instantly. Android limits what apps can do in the background to save battery, so a new photo can take a little while to appear. Nothing is ever lost — it always catches up.

    To make backups happen sooner: plug the phone in to charge (Android lifts its background limits while charging), stay on home Wi‑Fi, or open the PhotoSync app to trigger an immediate sync.

    Why haven't my latest photos backed up yet?

    Check that your phone is on home Wi‑Fi and this computer is on and running PhotoSync Server. Backups run periodically and resume on their own — you can also tap "Sync now" in the phone app.

    Can I delete a photo from both my phone and the server at once?

    Yes — long-press any photo in the PhotoSync app to enter selection mode, then tap Trash. This removes it from your phone and tells the server to delete its copy too.

    The Trash action requires an active connection to your server (same home Wi‑Fi, this app running). If the phone can't reach the server, the Trash button will be grayed out. You can still use Delete from device to remove the local copy, but the server copy will remain. There is currently no automatic retry, so if the connection is lost mid-delete the server copy will not be cleaned up later.

    Can I add photos to the server manually, without using the phone app?

    Yes. PhotoSync Server stores photos as plain files on your PC, organised in folders by user and date — for example PhotoSync\Alex\2026\06\photo.jpg. You can copy any photo or video into that folder structure yourself, and the server will serve it normally.

    The one extra step: the server keeps an index of all known files to avoid duplicates during upload. Files you copy in manually aren't in that index yet, so they won't appear in the "Photos on server" view on your phone until you click Scan for new files in the Server section of Settings above. The scan is instant and safe to run as many times as you like — it only adds files that aren't already indexed.

    Folder layout: create a subfolder named after the person (Alex, Maria, etc.) inside the backup folder, then year and month subfolders inside that, and drop your files in. Example: PhotoSync\Alex\2026\06\IMG_1234.jpg. If you don't need per-person folders, you can put files directly in the year/month path (PhotoSync\2026\06\IMG_1234.jpg) and they'll be attributed to the default account.

    Is it really free?

    Yes. PhotoSync is free and open source, and because the storage is your own computer there's no subscription and no limit beyond your own drive. It's built by one person on evenings and weekends — if it's useful to you, a small tip helps keep it going (entirely optional).
